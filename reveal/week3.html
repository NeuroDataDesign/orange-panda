Building a BCI

Major Types

  • Invasive
    • Stimulation inside of brain
  • Semi-Invasive
    • Stimulation on surface of brain
      Non-Invasive
  • Non-Invasive
  • Record / Stimulate / Both

Brain Responses for BCI

  • Conditioned Responses
    • Classical
      • Dog / Food / Bell
    • Instrumental
      • Dog / Reward / Lever
    • Operant
      • ​Action resulting in reward is paired with activity of neuron
  • Summary

Single / networks of neurons can be modulated by coupling neural activity with external actions that result in reward

Population Activity

  • Represents movement
    • Neurons fire according:
      • ​Preferred direction of movement relative to actual direction
  • Allows for prosthetics controlled by motor cortex

Imagined and Cognitive Ability

  • Motor Imagery
    • Imagining a movement (ex. Cursor)
      • ​Improves if person receives feedback while learning
  • Cognitive Task
    • Tied to a function
      • Relies on accurately discriminating activity patterns of cog. tasks

Stimulus – Evoked Activity

  • P300 Response
    • Pos deflection of EEG signal ~300ms after a stimulus
    • Event-Related (evoke) Potential
      • ​Caused by rare / unexpected stimulus
  • N100 and N400
    • Steady State Visually Evoked Potential
      • Neurons in visual cortex
      • When subject is fixating on stimulus

Stimulus – Evoked Activity

  • N100-P200 Complex
    • Caused by unpredictable stimulus
    • Followed by a positive wave (P200)
  • N400
    • ​Incongruent but meaningful inputs
    • Similar to error potential

Invasive BCI – Operant Conditioning

  • Neuron can be trained to control a derive
    • Monkey | Analog Dial | Reward | Firing Rate
  • Pros
    • No complicated machine learning algorithms needed
  • Cons
    • Training time can be long
  • Co-adaptive BCI
    • Brain + BCI adapt to speed up control of acquisition
    • Direct conversion of single cortical cells to a control signal

Invasive BCI – Population Decoding

  • Decode control signals
    • Neuron’s activation during movement
    • Direction
      • Neurons fire according to preferred direction (p) relative to actual direction (d)
      • Neuron’s firing rate (r)
      • Baseline firing rate (r0)
      • Max AVG. firing rate (rmax)
