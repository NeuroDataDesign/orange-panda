Cochlear Implants

  • Sound pressure waves hit tympanic membrane
  • Converted to mechanical vibrations by bones
    • Malleus
    • Incus
    • Stapes

Cochlear Implants

  • Mechanical vibrations transformed into pressure variations
    • In fluid filled cavity of cochlea
  • Cause displacements of flexible membrane
    • Basilar membrane
  • Hair cells attached to basilar membrane deflect
    • Causes neurons of cochlear nerve to fire

Key Component

  • Decomposes input sound into component frequencies
    • Different frequencies cause max vibrations at different locations
      • In Basilar membrane

High Frequency Sound Waves

  • Don't propagate far along membrane
    • Cause max displacement near stapes

Low Freq. Sound Waves

  • Max displacement at apex of basilar membrane
  • Frequency-to-place mapping (tonotopic)

Implants

  • Stimulate cochlear nerves directly
    • Use electrical impulses
    • Exploits tonotopic organization of nerves
  • Mimics function of lost or absent hair cells

Components

  • Microphone
    • Near ear
  • Signal processor
    • Externally behind year
    • Fast Fourier transform
      • Decomposes sound into freq. components
  • Transmitter
    • Externally
    • Transmits power and sound signals to internal receiver using radio frequency

Components

  • Receiver / stimulator
    • Behind ear, in skull
    • Convert received signals into electric pulses
    • Transmits to electrodes via cable
  • Electrode array
    • 22 electrodes max
    • Would up / placed along cochlea
    • Delivers electrical pulses to nerve fibers at different locations along cochlea

Details

  • Customization
  • Requires post-implantation therapy
  • 22 electrodes vs. 20,000 hair cells
    • Speech is understandable
    • Music, not so much

Applications

  • Works better with those who were born with hearing, but lost it
  • Works with people born deaf, however:
    • Closer implantation to birth = better results

Sensory Augmentation

Augmentation Basics

  • Several types of sensor signals combine to stimulate
    • Potential examples: infrared, ultrasound
  • Subject solves tasks based on these sensors, the brain eventually adapts
  • We can attach more sensors than available via evolution

Laboratory of Sur, MIT (2000)

  • Surgically diverted visual input to auditory input pathways in neonatal ferrets
    • Retinal axons -> medial geniuclate nucleus
  • Removed the auditory inputs

Results

  • Primary auditory cortex developed functional features of visual cortex
    • 2-D maps of visual space
    • Selective to orientation of visual stimuli and directional motion

Results (continued)

  • Rewired auditory cortex could solve visual tasks
  • Trained to move left for following sounds, right for light
  • One hemisphere of brain, vision, and sound was left unaltered as a control
    • Control hemisphere proved test worked and used to train the mice
  • Rewired hemisphere produced correct response
  • Proved brain is plastic, adapts to different types of input signals

Cursor Control: Direct Cortical Control + Stimulation

  • Direct intracortical implant to instruct monkey which target to move to, using joystick or direct brain control
  • 2 direct cortical implants (technically 2 recording arrays, 1 stimulating):
    • Stimulate somatosensory cortex
    • Record primary motor cortex
  • Train monkey via vibrations in joystick or stimulation in cortex: w/ stimulation move right, w/o move left
  • Successful results; after initial movement of joystick could predict cursor movement on motor cortex. Monkey learned stimulation patterns.

Experiment Diagram

Active Tactile Exploration

  • "Tactile information" delivered via stimulation to instruct monkey on cursor movement
  • Monkeys were trained to move a virtual arm on a screen to try and choose a target
  • Again stimulated somatosensory, recorded primary motor

Results

  • Different stimulations were sent to monkey's head if current target was correct or incorrect
  • Learned quickly which was right and which was wrong, started moving away from wrong one's quickly
  • Allowed brain control to move virtual arm after predictive model was generated
  • Performance increased to the point monkeys could tell whether a target was correct or not via stimulation in less than a second

RAT = Reward, UAT = Unreward (wrong target signal), NAT = Neutral (no signal)
