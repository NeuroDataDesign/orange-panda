</ul>

<h2><span>Threshold Model of Spike Generation</span></h2>
</div></div>
<div class="sl-block" data-block-type="text" style="width: 800px; left: 80px; top: 338px; height: auto;" data-block-id="9bdcb6efbf626c5ed0143f65adc1d2d7"><div class="sl-block-content" data-placeholder-tag="p" data-placeholder-text="Text">
<ul>
<li>Neuron receives sufficiently strong inputs from synapses for membrane to cross a specific threshold
<ul>
<li>Results in spike</li>
</ul>
</li>
<li>0/1 inputs are converted to analog changes in local membrane potential
<ul>
<li>Changes are summed at the soma</li>
<li>If summation exceeds threshold -&gt; Spike occurs</li>
</ul>
</li>
</ul>

<h2><span>Synaptic Plasticity</span></h2>

<ul>
<li>Long Term Potentiation (LTP)
<ul>
<li>Related to Hebbian Learning</li>
<li>If neuron A consistently causes neuron B to fire, then the strength of the connection from A to B should be increased</li>
<li>Hippocampus and neocortex</li>
</ul>
</li>
<li>Long Term Depression (LTD)
<ul>
<li>Decrease in strength of synaptic connection, caused by uncorrelated firing between two neurons</li>
<li>Hippocampus and neocortex</li>
</ul>
</li>
</ul>

<ul>
<li>Hebbian STDP
<ul>
<li>Presynaptic spike occurs (1-40ms) before postsynaptic spike à Strengthened synapse</li>
<li>Vise Versa (1-40ms after)</li>
<li>Cerebral cortex / Hippocampus</li>
</ul>
</li>
<li>Anti-Hebbian STDP
<ul>
<li>Presynaptic spike occurs after post synaptic spike -&gt; Strengthened synapse</li>
<li>Vise Versa</li>
<li>Inhibitory synapses in cerebellum</li>
</ul>
</li>
</ul>
