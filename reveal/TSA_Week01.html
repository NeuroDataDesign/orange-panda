Two Approaches to TSA

  • Time Domain
    • Model future value of time series as parametric function of currect/past values
  • Frequency Domain
    • Relates periodic or systematic sinusoidal variations found naturally in data

Spectral Analysis

  • Evaluate the variance associated with each periodicity of interest
  • Power Spectrum = Variance profile over frequency

Autocovariance Function

  • Measures linear dependence between 2 points on same series observed at different times
  • Average cross product relative to joint distribution F(x(s), t(s))

Autocovariance of White Noise

Autocovariance of a Moving Average

  • Only depends on time separation (lag)
    • Not on absolute location of points along series

Autocovariance of a Random Walk

  • Depends on particular time values (s and t)
    • Not on time separation or lag
  • Increases without bound as time t increases

Autocorrelation Function

  • Measures linear predictability of series at time t, using only the value x(s)

Strictly Stationary Time Series

  • Probabilistic behavior of every collection of values is identical to that of the time shifted set

Weakly Stationary Time Series

  • Mean value function is constant
    • Doesn't depend on time (t)
  • Autocovariance function depends on s and t only through their difference |s - t|
