EEG Alpha Asymmetry in Schizophrenia, Depression, PTSD, Panic Disorder, ADHD, and Conduct Disorder

Quick Summary

Abstract

  • Biomarker: models of laterality

    • Via EEG alpha asymmetry

  • Biomarker requires a more fine-grained assessment of clinical utility

  • Paper assesses resting brain laterality across 6 disorders

    • ~ 1900 dataset from Brain Resource International Database

    • Frontocentral region

    • Eyes closed and eyes open conditions

What's being looked at

  • Lateralization of neural activity associated with range of clinical disorders

    • Also emotional patterns in healthy people

    • Can be monitored via EEG alpha asymmetry

  • Brain at rest made up of neural networks

  • Baseline conditions = context for functioning of specific networks

Known Lateralization

  • Schizophrenia

    • Associated with abnormality of left hemisphere

    • Deficits in interhemispheric communication

    • Greater left lateralized alpha power

  • Depressive disorders

    • Specific lateralized dysfunction

    • Additional shift along anterior-posterior axis

    • This study found alpha asymmetry in right, but other studies haven't

Explaining Differing Laterality Findings in Depression

  • Subtypes of anxiety symptoms = different disorders

  • Anxiety disorders

    • PTSD: left lateralized parietal alpha

    • Panic Disorder: left lateralized global and frontal alpha

  • ADHD: Right lateralization of EEG alpha

    • Typically comorbid with ADHD have left lateralized front alpha power

Lateralization in Emotional Functioning

  • Healthy people

    • Reduced left alpha = positive emotion

    • Greater right = negative emotion

  • Males + females differ in laterality

    • Men show more right, women more left

    • Men typically have more

Results

  • Schizophrenia: greater left lateralized alpha power (than controls)

    • Deficit in left frontal activity at rest

  • Depression: more right-lateralized activity

  • Remaining disorders did not show difference from controls

    • Indicates may see some variance for other diseases in either left or right, but not too far from normal

  • Sex difference only in over 70 years old patients

  • Also has large dataset analyzed (~1,900 from Brain Resource International Database)
