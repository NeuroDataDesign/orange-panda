Medical EEG Study of Data Descriptors

Abstract

  • Multimodal assessment and analysis
    • Tailored to identify clinical subgroups
    • Validate established diagnostic boundaries

Common Limitation

  • Focus on delineation of single cognitive deficit
    • To explain all symptomatology
  • In-effective because for example
    • Processing speed imparement is a characteristic of both:
      • ADHD
      • Dyslexia

Data Recorded

  • Related to:
    • Processing speed
    • Basic sensory processing
    • Working memory
    • Attention

Conceptualizing Targets for Assessment

  1. Sensory Perception
  2. Integration of Information
  3. Response generation

Task Related Paradigms

Additional Insight

  • Standardized, abbreviated tests
    • Intelligence
    • Academic achievement
  • Self-reported psychiatric functioning
  • Study cog. performance / perception
    • Analyze variations in performance
      • From healthy to pathological functioning

Examination of Network Responses

  • No stimulation
    • Classical resting-state
  • Simple and reduced
    • Surround-suppression paradigm
  • Complex and rich
    • Videos
  • Probe relative levels of:
    • Attention / excitement / inhibition in visual cortex

Data

  • High-density task based (and free) EEG
  • Eye-tracking
  • Cognitive / behavioral data
  • 126 subjects
    • Ages 6 - 44

Overview

  • Participants recruited from:
    • Child Mind Medical Practice / NYC community
    • 80.2% developing
    • 19.8% diagnosed with clinical disorder(s)

Participant Diversity

  • 54.8 % male / 45.2 % female
    • 45.2 % African American
    • 32.7 % Caucasian
    • 0.04 % Asian
    • 12.7 % Other

Prior to Arrival

  • 10 min pre-screening phone interview
    • Psychiatric history
    • Current medications
    • Current / Past psychiatric treatment
    • Various neurological disorders
  • Approved if no history of:
    • Seizures / epilepsy / acute psychiatric symptoms

Overall Test

  • 3 hours of EEG and eye-tracking
  • 2 hours of cognitive / behavioral assessment
  • Approved by Chesapeake Institutional Review Board

Data Acquisition Overview

  • Participants seated in a silent, dark room
    • 70cm from a 17-inch monitor
  • Task instructions presented on screen
  • Alone in the room
    • Unless <12 years old - joined by proctor

EEG Acquisition

  • Used appropriately sized EEG nets
    • Impedance below 40 kOhm
    • Less than 30 min preparation
  • High-density EEG data
    • 500 Hz sampling rate
    • 0.1 - 100 Hz bandpass
    • 128-channel EEG Geodesic Hydrocel System
    • Recording reference at vertex of head

Eye Tracking Acquisition

  • Eye position and pupil dilation
    • Infrared video-based eye tracker
    • iView-X Red-m, SMI GmbH
    • 120 Hz sampling rate
    • 0.1 degree spatial resolution
    • Calibrated with a 5-point grid
      • Before each paradigm

EEG and Eye-tracking Paradigms

  • Task-free paradigms
    • Examine intrinsic functional network responses
      • Based on different degrees of stimulation
  • Talk-based paradigms
    • Isolate distinct information processing steps
  • Quantification of neurophysiological mechanisms underlying domain-general cognitive function

Passive Paradigms

  • Resting-State Paradigm

Task Overview

  • Resting state data is reliable (studies were cited)
    • State biological marker for individual brain activity
    • Can be related to cognitive performance

Stimuli & Experimental Design

  • Participants viewed cross in center of screen
  • Female research assistant instructs them to:
    • Open eyes - rest with eyes open for 20 s
    • Close eyes - rest for 40 s
    • Repeated 5 times

Output Measures

  1. Frequency domain
    • Classical power spectral analysis
  2. Temporal-spatial domain
    • Stable spatial configurations of electric field

Passive Paradigms

  • Sound Suppression Paradigm

Task Overview

  • Steady State Visually Evoked Potential
    • Signals from continuous oscillatory responses
    • Evoked by flickering stimuli
    • Proved useful for collecting data on:
      • Schizophrenia, anxiety, stress, epilepsy

Stimuli & Experimental Design

  • Flickering foreground stimuli w/ static surround patterns
  • 4 vertically-oriented circular gratings
    • Flickered at 25 Hz
    • Static-full screen
    • Peripheral disks w/ 2 degree radius
  • Upper disks flickered with opposite temporal phase relative to lower disks
    • Oscillatory summation on the scalp

Experimental Procedure

  • Fixation spot presented for 500ms
  • Foreground / surround stimuli were simultaneously presented for 2400 ms
    • Sinusoidal luminance-modulated gratings
    • Spatial frequency of 1cpd
  • 4 foreground contracts (0, 30, 60, 100)%
  • 3 surround conditions (parallel, orthogonal, none)
  • Eye tracking was continually monitored
  • Recorded in two blocks
    • 64 trials each, lasting 3.6 minutes

Participant Instructions

  • “Just maintain fixation on the central spot at all times. Press to begin. First we have to measure the position of your eyes. Just follow with your eyes the circle:”

Output Measures

  • Flickering foreground
    • Elicits SSVEP in EEG over posterior scalp
    • At fundamental freq. of stimulation
    • Amplitude increases monotonically w/ foreground contract
  • Surround suppression
    • Relative reduction in SSVEP amplitude due to surround contract
