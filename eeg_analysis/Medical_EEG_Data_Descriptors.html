Passive Paradigms

  • Sound Suppression Paradigm

Task Overview

  • Steady State Visually Evoked Potential
    • Signals from continuous oscillatory responses
    • Evoked by flickering stimuli
    • Proved useful for collecting data on:
      • Schizophrenia, anxiety, stress, epilepsy

Stimuli & Experimental Design

  • Flickering foreground stimuli w/ static surround patterns
  • 4 vertically-oriented circular gratings
    • Flickered at 25 Hz
    • Static-full screen
    • Peripheral disks w/ 2 degree radius
  • Upper disks flickered with opposite temporal phase relative to lower disks
    • Oscillatory summation on the scalp

Experimental Procedure

  • Fixation spot presented for 500ms
  • Foreground / surround stimuli were simultaneously presented for 2400 ms
    • Sinusoidal luminance-modulated gratings
    • Spatial frequency of 1cpd
  • 4 foreground contracts (0, 30, 60, 100)%
  • 3 surround conditions (parallel, orthogonal, none)
  • Eye tracking was continually monitored
  • Recorded in two blocks
    • 64 trials each, lasting 3.6 minutes

Participant Instructions

  • “Just maintain fixation on the central spot at all times. Press to begin. First we have to measure the position of your eyes. Just follow with your eyes the circle:”

Output Measures

  • Flickering foreground
    • Elicits SSVEP in EEG over posterior scalp
    • At fundamental freq. of stimulation
    • Amplitude increases monotonically w/ foreground contract
  • Surround suppression
    • Relative reduction in SSVEP amplitude due to surround contract

Output Measures

  • Naturalistic audiovisual stimuli- highly reliable neural activity across multiple viewers

  • Inter-subject correlation(ISC)

    • Measure of correlation across subjects

    • Successful memory encoding and effective communication between individuals

    • Increased during scenes of high arousal and negative emotional valence

    • Strongest for familiar and naturalistic events

  • Looking for similarity of neural response to stimuli in every-day life

  • EEG data analyzed using Correlated Component Analysis (CCA)

Correlated Component Analysis (CCA)

  • Identifies linear combinations of electrodes(called components) that maximize correlation across subjects
    • Like PCA, only instead of looking for max variance, max correlation

  • 3 strongest components are usually enough to explain most of the correlation, so paper limits sum of correlation to first 3 components

CCA Method

  • 2 matrices are calculated

    • Pooled between-subject covariance

    • Pooled within-subject covariance

  • Eigenvectors with highest eigenvalues are data projection that capture largest correlation. Do this for each component and sum them

    • Finding correlation of i-th component

    • Correlation = sum of components

Active Paradigms

Contrast Change Detection Paradigm

Task Overview

  • Recent study employed novel human EEG paradigm to isolate decision signal on human scalp

    • Determines timing of perceptual decisions vis discrete boundary-crossing criterion

    • Paper uses a modified version

  • Goal: examine neural determinants of simple perceptual decisions

    • Isolate discrete freely evolving neural signatures of 3 stages (sensory perception, integration of info, response generation)

Paradigm Overview

  • 2 Components:

    • Momentary encoding of sensory info

      • Rapidly flickering

    • Sequential integration of evidence into a decision variable

      • Constantly track motor prep

  • Advantages to paradigm

    • Gradual stimuli reduce sudden muscle movement and noise

    • Performed continuously, study sustained attention

Stimuli & Experimental Design

  • Isolate neural signatures of sensory evidence and accumulation directly

  • Minimum signal processing

  • Circular stimulus continuously flickering checkerboard pattern

  • Participants had to discriminate between 2 orientations of diagonal stripes on checkboard

    • Computer mouse clicks

      • Left-clicked if left pattern stronger, right-clicked vice versa

  • Feedback via smiling or sad face

  • Left tilt board: 25 Hz flicker, Right tilt: 20 Hz

    • Participants didn't distinguish

  • Switched up inter-trial intervals to avoid habituation/anticipation

  • 3 blocks of 24 trials

Output Measures

  • Sensory- Continuous flickering(SSVEP latency and amplitude)

    • Looked for a boundary-crossing to determine behavior

  • Motor response quantified as motor-related beta activity over electrodes C3 and C4

  • Additional measures of interest were reaction time, accuracy

Sequence Learning Paradigm

Task Overview

  • Evaluates neural correlates of declarative learning on an explicit visual sequence-learning task with semantically unloaded stimuli

  • Participants memorized given sequence of elements

  • Enables tracking process from unknown to fully memorized

  • Used sequence of simplified stimuli for most focused results

Stimuli & Experimental Design

  • Participants viewed sequence of 10 stimuli, appearing at 8 different locations

  • Repeated 5 times, then participant had to reproduce the sequence

  • Floor effect in pilot study

    • < 9 years old, abbreviated sequence 8 stimuli, 6 locations

  • No restrictions on response time, subjects focused on a central fixation cross

Output Measures

  • Trials categorized by

    • "Hits"

    • "Misses"

    • "Learned"

  • P300 came 300 ms after novel or unexpected stimuli

    • Independent of motor response and sensory stimuli

WISC-IV Symbol Search/Processing Speed Paradigm

Task Overview

  • Examined neuro- and psychophysiological mechanisms underlying processing speed in a visual search task

  • Processing Speed

  • Sensitive

  • Previous Studies

WISC-IV

  • Processing speed measured using Symbol Search subtest of Wechsler Intelligence Scale for Children IV (WISC-IV)

    • Assess information processing speed

    • Visual processing

    • Requires subject to solve as many trials possible in 2 minutes

    • Adapted from paper to computer

  • Subject must

    • Detect and encode symbol

    • Find matching symbol in probes

    • Encode pairing symbols

    • Select proper response

    • Initiate and execute response

In this paradigm...

  • Studies strategies that may decrease performance, such as double checking

  • Present approach (EEG + eye tracking) allows decomposing task into interpretable components of processing

Stimuli & Experimental Data

  • Stimuli consisted of black symbols 1 sq. cm

  • 15 trials at same time on screen

    • 4 pages of these

    • No one ever reached the end of the 60 total trials

  • Participants shown rows containing 5 symbols, searched for 2 target symbols in each row and indicated if there or not

Output Measures

  • Number score on test of correct

  • Time spent looking at each symbol

  • Number of saccade steps

  • Number of repetitions

  • Pupil size

  • Protracted gaze dwell times for each sub-region of the screen

  • Can also use this data to identify strategies used in test and reasons for actual score

  • Eye-tracking can be complemented with EEG data

EEG and Eye-Tracking Preprocessing Steps

  • preprocessed by MATLAB
  • triggers and latencies extracted and specific temporal order of patients determined
  • replace and identify bad electrodes (electrodes that are > 3 stdev away from other electrodes in frequency spectrum distribution) using spherical spline interpolation (?)
  • noisy channels filtered - either visually or replaced entirely by zeros
  • 109 channels for EEG, 6 EOG for artifact removal

More Preprocessing

  • data high pass filtered at 0.1 Hz and notch filtered at 59-61 Hz
  • Principal Components Analysis (PCA) algorithm removed sparse noise - recovers a low-rank matrix from a corrupted matrix by subtracting the error.
  • visual inspection after preprocessing as well.

Tools for EEG Analysis

  • EYE-EEG: plugin for MATLAB- integrates analysis for electrophysiological + eye-tracking data.
  • adds eye-tracking data as extra channels to EEG
  • Adaptive velocity-based algorithm to detect saccades and fixations, i.e. a blink
    • identifies fixation as groups of consecutive points
    • uses that to map minimum and maximum x and y values - compare that to maximum dispersion value.
    • if the dispersion is lower, then its a fixation.

  • analyze eye-closed

  • Resting-state EEG had their spectral amplitude saved.
  • Spectra for each channel averaged for the epochs
  • group mean spectral amplitude for all or each electrode calculated

Sequence Learning Paradigm

  • EEG data segmented based on stimulus of each filled white circles at each of the eight different locations
  • 900 ms long epochs (100 ms pre, 800 ms post)
  • averaged trial types (hits, missed, learned) for each subject individually and calculated group average
  • found a decrease of P300 over different blocks
  • behavioral performance: percentage of correctly learned spatial location
  • learning rate: newly learned location in relation to all possible locations

More Sequence Learning Paradigm

  • P300 amplitude = amplitude on electrode CPZ, with latency of 350-500 ms post stimulus
  • slightly delayed because children have greater latency
  • performance increases while learning rate and P300 decrease w practice
  • recall the expectation that subjects learn to expect the order of the appearance of targets w/ 5 blocks
  • decrease in P300 because of novelty indicator of a target stimulus- subjects are not surprised anymore!

Surround Suppression Paradigm

  • extracted based on flickering stimulus
  • 4 foreground contrast (0%, 30%, 60%, 100%) and 3 background conditions (parallel, orthogonal, none)
  • data of two blocks merged for each subject
  • computed SSVEP signal without background, and a SSVEP averaged all conditions with a background
  • FFT computed to obtain a measure of SSVEP power at 25 Hz freq
  • subtracted this from neighboring freq to get the actual evoked activity

More on Surround Suppression Paradigm

  • in-house algorithm to detect highest SSVEP amplitude, compute signal based on the average of max electrode + four surrounding electrodes
  • graph to demonstrate the difference in SSVEP amplitude between different contrasts with or without a background
  • increase SSVEP amplitude with an increase in foreground contrast
  • relative reduction in SSVEP amplitude due to surround contrast

Contrast Change Detection Paradigm

  • data of three blocks merged for each subject
  • target epochs extracted from 500 ms before target to 1000 ms after peak sensory evidence
  • response-locked epochs extracted from 1000 ms before repsonse to 300 ms after response (buttons)
  • rejected trials for scalp channel over 100 microV
  • SSVEP based on stimulus locked epochs + motor response signal based on response-locked epochs measured by Fourier
  • CPP analysis averages single-trial waveforms (baseline corrected 500 ms before response)

More on Contrast Change Detection Paradigm

  • Figure to show SSVEP, CPP, and motor response signal topographies
  • Result shows posterior maximum for SSVEP around electrode Oz.
  • CPP shows highest activity near CPz electrode and motor response signal peaks over C3 and C4.
