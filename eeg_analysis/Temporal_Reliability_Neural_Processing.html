Audience Preferences Predicted by Temporal Reliability of Neural Processing

Observation

  • Stimuli that we judge favorably
    • Our Brains respond to in a stereotypical manner shared by our peers

Previous Investigations

  • Neural correlations of individual:
    • Preferences
    • Subjective Values
    • Choices
  • Using fMRI while people performed experimental tasks

Hypothesis

  • Level of neural reliability elicited by naturalistic stimulus (in small sample)
    • Predictive of behavioral responses reflecting engagement or interest of a large population

Procedure

  • 12-16 naive subjects presented with previously aired stimuli (aggregated measures of pop. response)
    • EEG and fMRI
  • Found statistically significant link between neural reliability in sample and preferences of large audiences
    • Contemporary audiovisual stimuli

Conclusion

  • Behavioral responses of large groups to natural stimuli may be robustly predicted from the neural responses of a small sample of individuals

Results

  • Premiere broadcast of "The Walking Dead"
    • 16 participants repping the target demographic
    • Particioned into 190 constituent scenes
  • Twitter traffic sorted for time-stamped, stimulus-relevant tweets from original airing

Additional Factors Considered

  • Frequency of tweets per scene
  • Tweets are more delayed than EEG signals
  • Neilsen ratings
    • Sudden drop during advertisements
    • Declining audience retention
    • Opportunity to switch channels

Discussion

  • Behavioral responses of large audiences correlate to the neural responses of a small group of individuals
  • Have not:
    • Concluded that neural signals explain the population response better than the response of the sample
  • Information comes from temporal dynamics
    • Not strength of responses

Discussion

  • Twitter / Nielson ratings
    • Indicative of interest, not (dis)/likability
  • Conclude:
    • Naturally occurring audience behaviors can be forcasted from scalp measurements

EEG Data Collection

  • Stimuli in darkened, electrically / acoustically shielded room
  • 64 electrode cap
    • 10/10 international EEG standard
    • 512 Hz frequency
    • 24 bits / sample
  • Recorded eye-movements
    • EOG with 4 electrodes
      • 1 adjacent / 1 below each eye

EEG Preprocessing

  • EEG/EOG data
    • High-pass filtered - 1Hz cutoff
    • Notch filtered at 60 Hz
    • Down sampled to 256 Hz
  • Eye tracking data
    • Linearly regressing 4 EOG channels
    • Channels whose average power exceeded mean channel power by 4 stdev. was excluded
    • Samples whose squared amplitude exceeded mean-squared-amplitude of the channel were excluded

Neural Reliability Analysis

  • Neural reliability elicited by given stimulus
  • Dmochowski component analysis
