A Possible EEG Marker for Engagement

Introduction

  • Regress neural activity of one subject into that of another
    • Correlation between multiple records to inform state variables
    • fMRI studies show strong voxel-wise inter-subject correlations across participants
      • Exposed to common naturalistic stimulus
  • fMRI tells us if neural activity significantly correlates in response to a certain stimuli
  • Cannot tell us when this synchronization occurs\
  • Cannot analyze fast oscillatory activity

fMRI

EEG

  • Measures correlations to ongoing oscillatory activity
  • Derive a signal decomposition method
    • Finds linear components of data with max mutual correlations (in time)

Investigate

  • Link between neural correlation and viewer engagement
    • Applications in:
      • Neuro-marketing
      • Quantitative assessment of entertainment
      • Impact of narrative discourse
      • Study of ADHD

What They've Observed

  • Peaks in neural correlation occur in high correspondence with arousing moments in the film
    • Don't arise in amateur footage of daily life
    • Fewer peaks the second time watching
    • Fewer peaks when watched out of order
  • Alpha and beta bands decrease during synchronous peaks
    • Theta power increased during windows of heightened correlation
  • Engagement can be measured to the 1-second mark

Extraction of Maximally Correlated Components

  • Algorithm maximizes Pearson Product Moment Correlation Coefficient
    • Simultaneously diagonalizes the pooled covariance and cross-correlations of 2 data sets
  • Linear components that achieve this
    • Solutions of a generalized eigenvalue equation

Procedure

  • 20 subjects (normal vision/hearing)
  • 14 males / 6 females
    • 21 - 45 years old
  • Instructed to watch clips, and refrain from moving
    • Three 6-minute clips, each shown twice
    • Used control film : natural outdoor scene

Data Collection / Pre-Processing

  • 64 electrode cap
    • 512 Hz freq.
    • 10/10 international standard
  • EOG (to remove eye-movement artifacts)
    • 4 electrodes
  • Processing performed offline (MATLAB)

Data Collection / Pre-Processing

  • High-pass filtered (0.5 Hz)
  • Notch filtered (60 Hz)
  • Linearly regress out 4 EOG channels
    • Remove eye-movement artifacts
  • EEG samples w/ squared magnitudes above 4 stdev. of mean power of respective channel
    • Replaced with 0

Statistical Significance

  • Permutation Test Approach
    • Compute correlations with one of two records
      • 2nd viewing or subject
      • Scrambled in time
    • 2nd record is divided into 5-s blocks
      • Order of blocks randomly shuffled
  • Significance tests are corrected for multiple comparisons using the false discovery rate

Results

Peaks in Intra-Subject Correlations

  • Found there is a significant increase in proportion of significantly correlated time windows in the two critically acclaimed films, as compared to the control film
  • When order of clip was shuffled, fewer correlations
  • When watched a second time, fewer correlations

Alpha / Theta Waves

  • Desynchronization of alpha band
    • Increased attention
  • Increased alpha-band oscillations
    • Attention suppression
  • Compared instantaneous power at several EEG freq. bands
    • High and low within subject correlations

Alpha / Theta Waves

  • Increased Theta band power
    • Highly correlated times
  • Synchromization of frontal theta power with decrease in alpha power
    • Linked to encoding new info

Discussion

  • Clear overlap between engagement / attention
    • Both involve suppression of internally oriented mental processing
  • Engagement entails emotional involvement
  • Increased theta activity in frontal area
    • Implicated with memory encoding
  • Correlated Component Analysis applied
    • Did not prove effective
  • Further investigations may require ICA-type algorithm
    • Independent component analysis

Conclusion

  • It is possible to identify ongoing attentional and emotional changes using temporally localized changes in neural synchrony
  • The context of the stimuli was very important
  • Projections of data that maximize correlations across viewings will reflect more of the sensory processing, and less of the default-mode activity
    • Than that of raw recordings

Conclusion

  • Neural activity of less attentive viewer
    • Exhibits less of the extrinsic response and more intrinsic activity (noise)
    • Decreased correlations
  • Sensory processing becomes more precisely time-locked to stimulus during high engagement
